# The Cover Slide

*   Get the basics right — make sure this has your <mark>company name, logo, website and social media handles.</mark>
*   Use imagery where appropriate — a picture that sums up what you do here would be good.
*   Have you got <mark>partners or won any awards?</mark> Add that detail here.

> <mark>Steer clear of putting a date here, it will soon make your deck look like an old version.</mark>

# The Impact Slide

This slide is a <mark>short concise summary</mark> of what you do and <mark>your mission</mark>. It should be intriguing and culturally relevant.

What standout elements are there to your business that an investor should see upfront? Think the <mark>‘5Ms’</mark>:
*   **Momentum** (traction — revenue, month of month growth, MRR, other KPIs)
*   **Market** (how big)
*   **Machine** (tech — why specialise)
*   **Management** (rock star team — prior startup experience, done it before)
*   **Money** (how much raising)

> **Remember the person reading this might not know your industry — lose any jargon.**

# Why you exist

*   **What pain point are you solving?** Ensure this problem comes across as <mark>mission critical</mark> — state any user and market research you have to back up claims.

*   Be clear <mark>if this is a new problem your customers are dealing with or an old problem</mark> where you plan to bring a new/disruptive solution

*   Normally <mark>if an investor gets involved, it is because of one of the following:</mark>
    *   They have personally experienced the problem in the past
    *   There is a clear sense of ROI for them
    *   Given their professional expertise, they **understand it.**

> **Sometime problems can be too abstract — address this by personalising it.**

# Ta-dah!

*   **Use this slide to reveal why things are better now that problem has been solved.**
*   Focus on the **business benefits of the solution** and what they allow the customer to do, not just what the product does. Do you **save people time? Money? Generate new revenue?** Explain why it is compelling to your customers
*   The solution needs to be **graphically/ visually represented** — show screenshots
*   Use **user and market research** to highlight benefits that lead into "Competitive Advantage"

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Remember how many decks we get every year — make sure yours doesn't start to get woolly.**

# Now let's get into it

*   Represent and **define the market** you operate in
*   Explain the market and sub-sectors
*   Highlight the size of the **market (and therefore opportunity)**, and historical / forecast market growth. This should be backed up with solid market research
*   **Use your current revenues** to show your market share, and forecast what share you could obtain in 3 – 5 years
*   **Be realistic** i.e. if you are selling widgets, your market size is not the total industrial retail market, only the widget market

**Include images or diagrams to simplify what you're saying here**

# Tech & product

*   The investors looking at you may not necessarily share your level of expertise, so simply explain the technology you use
*   What is the technology/ product that you've built? <mark>Why is it special/ cutting edge?</mark>
*   What is the <mark>secret sauce?</mark>
*   What is the IP? Are there any <mark>patents</mark> in the business?
*   What are the <mark>regulatory considerations?</mark> (If a regulated business, separate slide on regulations)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Include images or diagrams to simplify what you're saying here**

# The team

Business is personal — introduce <mark>the key people the investor is investing in.</mark>

Highlight any experience of working together previously; <mark>the founders’ chemistry</mark>

<mark>Why is this the right team</mark> to execute the opportunity? Highlight any experience of working in an early stage business, scaling/exiting a business

Spend time getting this right and present them in a professional and consistent style. If you need <mark>professional headshots</mark> — get them now.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Can you include individual achievements for your team? This will help them stand out.**

# The drill-down

*   The operating/revenue model of the business i.e. how do you make your money?
*   Ensure business model is backed up by <mark>solid calculations and research</mark>
*   <mark>Explain fee structure and the interaction between different revenue streams</mark> – if possible, quantify this as currently revenue or % of current revenue/include unit economics
*   <mark>Graphically/visually represent</mark> the business model

> Financials will form another slide later on so focus here on the unit economics and how you make money.

# Go-to-market

Explain your <mark>go-to-market strategy</mark>, highlighting:
* your **customer acquisition strategy**; how have you acquired customers. So far and how will that change?
* How do customers find you (**key sales and marketing channels**) and what are the customer acquisition costs for these channels?
* How do you convert leads and what are your conversion rates?
* What are your plans for sales and marketing going forward

What have you done to date and <mark>how successful has this been</mark> (evidence through KPIs, CAC vs LTV etc)

<mark>Use visuals to illustrate your go-to-market strategy</mark>

# Know your competition

*   Acknowledge competitors and <mark>show points of difference from your market research</mark>
*   Discuss and <mark>evidence any barriers</mark> to entry/defensibility you hold over incumbents/competitors and future entrants (the barriers to entry might be a separate slide)
*   Use a matrix with traffic lights or quadrant box to <mark>highlight key features and differentiators</mark> between you and your competition

> **Of course- your product is better than the rest. Compare and contrast with the rest.**

# Financials

*   Summarise your <mark>historical and forecast profit and loss account</mark>, drawing out key metrics (MRR, no. of customers, customer churn, gross margins) — 2 year prior actuals, 5 year forecast
*   Show contracted revenue in the forecast period
*   Include a revenue bridge, <mark>bridging revenue to date to the full year forecast outturn</mark>
*   Highlight forecast cash burn and <mark>state when you expect to become profitable</mark> (i.e. breakeven)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Have you been affected by Covid 19 — include mitigations**

# Who else likes you?

*   Don't be shy about using <mark>third party praise</mark> to support your pitch. Including endorsements from well-known clients — companies or influencers — will add weight to your submission.
*   Highlight your <mark>current pipeline</mark>, weighted and unweighted by stage of opportunities
*   Show <mark>traction to date</mark> including stats such as members, users, revenue, margins, growth, engagement (ideally this should be monthly).
*   Highlight <mark>contracts signed and / or key clients</mark> including annual contract value if relevant.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Remember:**
> **this can all be verified, so don't make anything up.**

# The ask

*   Describe your funding history and <mark>the amount you're looking to raise</mark>
*   Who are your current investors and how much have you raised to date? <mark>Include names if these hold sway/ credibility in the industry</mark>
*   <mark>Explain your use of funds</mark> for the raise using a visual (make sure aligned to financial model) including key hires
*   Discuss <mark>key milestones</mark> (product, revenue, new markets) you plan to hit in the next 12-24 months

> [!WARNING]
> **Don't put a valuation here. We'll talk about valuation in another post but without the deal structure, it can be misleading.**

# The finale

Conclude your pitch here — <mark>highlight key points</mark> of the presentation.
