     Under 141(k) of the DGCL, directors generally may be removed with or without cause by the holders of a
majority of the voting power of the shares entitled to vote in an election of directors. Where directors, such as the
Preferred Director and Common Directors, are elected by the holders of a class or series, such directors may be
removed without cause only by the holders of a majority of the class or series that elected them. Removals for cause
can be effected by a majority of the shares entitled to vote in an election of directors. This model Voting Agreement
provides when and how the stockholder parties may and/or must vote to remove directors. In general, stockholders
may vote to remove a director with or without cause only if requested by the person(s) who are entitled to designate
the director. In a case where a director is designated by agreement of multiple parties, the request must come from
all of those parties unless otherwise specified in such agreement. This model Voting Agreement includes sample
language where a Mutual Director’s removal can be requested by vote of a majority of the Board, even though
designation of a Mutual Director requires mutual agreement of all other directors. The circumstances under which
directors can be removed, particularly where there may be cause for removal, should be carefully reviewed and
adjusted depending on the conditions set forth in Section 1.2 and the business understanding among the parties.
