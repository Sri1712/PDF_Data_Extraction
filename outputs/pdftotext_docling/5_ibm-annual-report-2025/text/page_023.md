Total selling, general and administrative (SG&A) expense increased 2.2 percent in 2025 versus 2024, driven primarily by the
following factors:

• Higher operating expenses from acquired businesses, as a result of our continued investment to drive our hybrid cloud and AI
  strategy (4 points); and

• Higher amortization of acquired intangible assets and acquisition-related charges (1 point); and

• The effects of currency (1 point); partially offset by

• Benefits from productivity and the actions taken to transform our operations (4 points).

Operating (non-GAAP) SG&A expense increased 1.0 percent year to year primarily driven by the same factors above, excluding the
higher amortization of acquired intangible assets and acquisition-related charges.

Expected credit loss expense was a provision of $33 million in 2025 as compared to a benefit of $21 million in 2024. The year-to-
year change was primarily driven by higher unallocated reserve requirements in the current year as a result of the current economic
conditions. Refer to “Receivables and Allowances” section on page 25 for additional information.
